## Models And Tools Used

### AI:
    1. ChatGPT Image Model - Used for Visual Assets
    2. Gemini Nano Banana 2 - Used for Visual Assets
    3. Gemini - Used for Audio Assets (Music)
    4. ElevenLabs - Used for Audio Assets (SFX)

### Non-AI
    1. GIMP (Image manipulation tool)
    2. Dazz's tSR Tools (Sprite tools)

## Prompts
See Asset logs for more details but here are sample prompts, with iterations:

### Sprite creation of idle.png (static sprite) using Open AI's ChatGPT:
- "a cyberpunk monkey, 2d cartoon style, idle pose - staring towards the screen, 256 by 256"
- "need full body, doing an A pose"
- "make him smile, with a bit of cheekiness"

### Sprite sheet creation of running animation (animated sprite) using ChatGPT
- "can you generate 8 images of it moving left in animated frames sense (individual images)"
- "can you remove the first monkey, then make alternate poses so it looks like he is running like one foot forward, then the next image is feet together"

### SFX creation of jump sound using ElevenLabs
- "jumping sound for a platformer"
- "long jumping sound for a platformer"

## Iterations
Sound assets like sound effects and music took the least amount of iterations, around 1 or 2 iterations. Sprite assets and backgrounds which are visual assets took more iterations with heavy post editing, ranging from 1 to 10 iterations.

## Notes
In conclusion, AI generated assets are not production ready from just generating them, they need multiple iterations as well as post edits. Assets that have dependencies like animations based sprites are the poorest quality in terms of AI generation. Static sprites and backgrounds are much easier to work with. The easiest ones to make were sound assets like sound effects and music but even they may need some touch ups.
